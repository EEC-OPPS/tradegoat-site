The full feature set · every feature in every tier

Everything it does —
the whole loop, one tool.

TradeGOAT runs the six things every service company does: price the work, propose it, win it, track the money, run the field, and learn from every job. Built trade-agnostic — it speaks your trade's language, not ours.

1

Price it — know your real cost

The margin-first engine: true costs in, one honest margin, price out. No phantom profit.

Cost-up calculator

Crew, days, materials, travel, hotels, permits — every real cost builds the price, itemized line by line.

Overhead inside the cost

Your overhead per job day is folded into cost before margin — so the profit you see is profit you keep.

One honest margin with guardrails

You set your own warning and danger floors; the slider shows them in color while you price.

Per-service rates

Your big-job rate and your service-call rate are different numbers — whatever your trade calls them, each service keeps its own.

Multiple options per proposal

Let the customer pick the scope instead of haggling the price.

Exact price override + minimum job fee

Need to hit a number? Lock it, and the tool shows you the margin you're accepting.

2

Propose it — your documents, alive

The headline act: bring the proposal you already use, and it becomes the system.

Bring-your-own-document ingestion

Upload your Word or PDF proposal — the tool reads your scope language, terms, warranty, and payment schedule and turns them into a living template.

The red-pen review

Every detected fill-in spot shown against your original words — you confirm or fix. Everything you don't mark is never touched.

Real Word output

Finished Proposals render to actual documents in your format, with your logo — editable, printable, emailable.

The safety gate

A Proposal physically cannot be sent with a blank fill-in spot a customer would see.

Your numbering

Simple sequential or your own custom format — per proposal type if you want it.

Frozen on signing

Signed Proposals lock forever. Changes become change orders — the original price is untouchable.

3

Win it — the pipeline that doesn't forget

Deals that take nine months don't get lost in month two.

The status spine

Draft → sent → under review → on hold → signed → lost, changeable right from the dashboard card, with every deal's age ticking in plain sight.

On Hold is first-class

Parked deals keep their place for as long as the customer's board takes.

The signature is the starting gun

Signing creates the job instantly — billing milestones from your own payment split, trackers running, money picture updated.

Every outcome teaches

Won and lost Proposals freeze their economics automatically, feeding the win/loss analysis with zero data entry.

4

Track the money — every dollar, honestly labeled

In hand, owed, committed, and coming — never blurred together.

Plain-English invoice ledger

Ready to send · out awaiting payment · paid. Three buckets, no accounting degree required.

Billing milestones per job

Your payment split (50/25/25 or whatever you run) with amounts, invoice numbers, and collected status per draw.

QuickBooks connection

Push invoices, read payments back, and mirror the books' real billing history onto every job. After you connect, QuickBooks is the billing truth.

The money-out side of every job

Parts, travel and hotels, and labor — each tracked in its own lane against what you bid, so a job's real cost is never a mystery.

Gina sorts your QuickBooks for you

She reads your expenses, groups them by vendor and job site, and hands you "these belong to this job" to approve in one tap. You enter your crew's labor; she pulls the rest from your books — and the profit you see is the real one.

Retainage tracking

Bill gross, watch the holdback per draw — for any trade that lives with retainage.

The owner's money view

Bank balance from your books, lifetime billed and collected, receivables vs backlog — labeled precisely, revealed on tap, gated person by person.

Company Insights

Your QuickBooks P&L rendered exactly as your bookkeeper sees it — income, spending, and net, year by year.

5

Run it — the field and the office, one system

Your paper ticket reborn on a phone; an office list that empties itself.

Your service ticket, digitized

Upload your own half-page ticket — Word, Excel, PDF, or a photo — and techs fill the same form on their phones.

Signed on glass

Photos, parts, hours, and the customer's signature captured on site; the finished PDF emails itself to the customer and the office.

Service clocks on everything

Every unit, property, machine, or site under contract carries a clock — finish a ticket and it resets.

The missed-visit watch

A maintenance window closing without a visit gets flagged before the customer notices. Ever.

Needs-billing list

Billable field work lands with hours, miles, and parts already totaled, and stays until the bill goes out. "Got it running, didn't take payment" — never again.

Scheduling & crews

Company calendar, personal schedules, time-off requests, per-job crew assignment.

Customers, vendors & equipment

One record per relationship — contacts, locations, equipment, contracts, history, lifetime money. Bulk-import all of it from spreadsheets or straight from QuickBooks with duplicate-proof matching.

It speaks your language

Units, properties, machines, roofs — you pick the word once and the whole tool uses it, everywhere.

6

Learn from it — the tool that makes you a better businessman

Most contractors find out what a job made at tax time. Not anymore.

Live job scorecard

Bid vs actual by cost bucket while the job runs — a slipping margin shows up when you can still steer it back into the green.

Won vs Lost

Your winning Proposals next to the ones that got away — margins, price per day, where you win by size and type. It builds itself from every outcome.

Qualification packet

Completed and in-progress work with amounts, dates, and types — the questions general contractors ask, printable in one click.

Business overview & customer reports

Revenue by type, top customers, win rate, collected vs backlog — exportable, printable.

Governed AI learning

Every lesson the tool learns about your business is visible, reviewable, and yours to pause or delete. Nothing teaches itself behind your back.

AI

Your AI office — Gina works in the background

An office secretary who reads, files, and dispatches — and never does anything behind your back.

Her own inbox

Forward the email that matters to Gina's address and it lands in her tray, ready to read and act on — your own inbox stays untouched.

Nothing happens without your tap

Gina stages everything — a document to file, a cost to sort onto a job — and waits. Nothing enters your books or your records until you approve it. She drafts; you decide.

Documents by email

Forward a PDF to a job's inbox and it lands in that job's Documents for the whole team — not buried in one person's email.

Dispatch from one board

Send a job to a crew member; their phone buzzes, they acknowledge, and it moves across the board until it's done.

The field experience is built for the truck, not shrunk from a desktop.
