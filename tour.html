Bring your own

Your proposal walks in the door. It doesn't start over.

Every tool you've tried wanted you to rebuild your business inside their template. TradeGOAT reads the Word or PDF proposal you already use — your scope language, your terms and conditions, your warranty, your payment schedule — and turns it into a living template. The fill-in spots become automatic. The rest is never touched.

  • Word in → editable Word out, in your format, ready to send
  • A red-pen review screen shows your original words with every fill-in spot marked — you confirm or fix, nothing sneaks by
  • A safety gate: a proposal physically cannot be sent with a blank fill-in spot
  • One document, ingested once — every future proposal in seconds

2
Bid it

Real costs. One honest margin. No phantom profit.

Most estimating tools let you lie to yourself — margin on labor, markup on materials, overhead nowhere. TradeGOAT's engine is margin-first: every real cost goes in (crew, days, parts, travel, even your overhead per job day), you set one profit margin, and the price is computed the way the math actually works.

  • Overhead lives inside the cost — the margin you see is money you actually keep
  • Per-service rates — your big-job rate and your service-call rate are different numbers, whatever your trade calls them — and the tool knows it
  • Multiple options per proposal — let the customer pick the scope, not haggle the price
  • Every bucket itemized — labor crews by name, equipment rentals by the day, permits by the line

Track it

Deals that take nine months don't get lost in month two.

Commercial work waits — on boards, budgets, and building owners. Your dashboard holds every proposal at its real stage: sent, under review, on hold, signed, lost — with the age of every deal ticking in plain sight. Move a deal between stages right from its card.

  • On Hold is a first-class state — parked deals keep their place for as long as it takes
  • Signed proposals lock forever — the customer's contract can never quietly change; changes become change orders
  • Win/loss history builds itself from every outcome
  • The owner's view — collected, billed, earned, pipeline: live money tiles only the right eyes see

4
Win it

The signature is the starting gun.

The moment a proposal is signed, the job exists: billing milestones are created from your own payment split, operational trackers start, and the dashboard's money picture updates. Waiting half a year for the deposit check? The paperwork doesn't wait with it.

  • Your payment schedule, not ours — each company defines its own milestone split in Settings
  • Deposit decoupled — recorded when it actually arrives, never a blocker
  • Change orders append — the original contract price is untouchable

The owner's view

What's in the bank. What's owed. What's coming. One glance.

Connect QuickBooks and the dashboard answers the owner's questions without a single report being run: the bank balance from your own books, everything billed and collected this year and lifetime, what's out awaiting payment, and the signed work you haven't billed yet.

  • Straight from QuickBooks — the same bank balance your bookkeeper sees, on your dashboard automatically
  • Receivable vs backlog, labeled honestly — "billed, awaiting payment" and "signed work left to bill" are different money, and the tool says so
  • You control who sees it — books visibility is granted person by person, even among owners
  • Hidden by default — one tap reveals, one tap hides

6
Run it in the field

Your paper service ticket — reborn on the tech's phone.

Every service company has that half-page ticket: one copy to the customer, one for the tech, one back to the office. Upload yours — Word, Excel, PDF, or a photo of the paper form — and TradeGOAT rebuilds it as the digital ticket your techs fill on site. Same fields. Same labels. Your form.

  • The tech types almost nothing — date, customer, address, phone fill themselves from the unit
  • Hours compute themselves — start, end, optional lunch window deducted
  • Photos + signatures on glass — customer signs on the phone, right there
  • Finalize & send — customer and office get the signed PDF instantly, and the ticket files itself to that unit's history forever

Never miss a beat

Service clocks, missed-visit watch, and a billing list that empties itself.

Everything you service under contract — every unit, property, machine, or site — carries a service clock. Finish a ticket and it resets; miss one and it's flagged before the customer notices. Every ticket a tech marks billable lands on the office's needs-billing list, with the hours, miles, and parts already totaled, until the bill goes out.

  • "Got it running, didn't take payment" — never forgotten again
  • Contract renewals surface early — with the price-increase terms you set
  • Per-unit service history — a clean, printable one-pager the customer can hold
  • Field techs see their day — the office sees everything

9
Learn from it

Know what the job really made — before it's over.

Most contractors find out what a job made at tax time. Every TradeGOAT job carries a live scorecard: what you bid next to what it's actually costing, category by category, with your true margin recalculating as actuals come in. When a number slips, you tag why — under-bid, delay, scope — while there's still time to steer the job back into the green.

  • Variance per bucket — see exactly where the money moved
  • Catch it while it's fixable — a slipping margin shows up mid-job, when you can still change course, not at tax time
  • Reason tags build a pattern — your next bid gets sharper than your last
  • Role-aware — the owner sees the P&L; the crew sees the schedule, not the dollars

10
It grows with you

Every Proposal you run through it makes the next one smarter.

The analytics aren't a report you buy — they're a picture that builds. Every win and every loss freezes its economics: price, margin, size, days on site. Week one, you get a baseline. Six months in, you know your win rate by job size, what your winning margins actually look like, and which bids you were right to lose. The tool doesn't know your business on day one — it learns it, the same way you did.

  • Lost bids are data, not failures — marking a loss freezes the numbers so the comparison stays honest
  • Won vs lost, side by side — margins, price-per-day, job size: see what your market says yes to
  • The picture sharpens with history — the more of your business runs through the tool, the more it can teach you
