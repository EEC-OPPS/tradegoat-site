Week of July 18, 2026

Your books and your jobs, finally in sync

  • Two-way QuickBooks billing. Bill a job milestone and TradeGOAT pushes a real QuickBooks invoice with the tax handled — the number and amounts mirror back onto the job. Record a payment in QuickBooks and the tool notices and matches it, so you're never double-entering.
  • Know what a job really cost. New money-out tracking puts parts, travel, and labor in their own lanes against what you bid. Gina reads your QuickBooks expenses and proposes which job each one belongs to for one-tap approval — so you see a job's true profit, not a guess. (You enter your crew's labor; she pulls the rest from your books.)
  • Parts payables. Track what you owe your parts vendor on each job, with QuickBooks payment matching. The tool reads your books — it never moves your money.
  • A money-first job page. Contract, collected, remaining, and money-out sit right at the top of every job.
  • Gina's Office. Your AI office secretary now has her own inbox and a "needs you" tray that triages the junk — and she can dispatch a job to a crew member, whose phone buzzes.
  • A cleaner, color-coded look across the whole app, so every number reads at a glance.
