Before You Buy Software for Your Trade Business: 5 Questions That Save You Months

By Travis Evans, Founder of TradeGOAT · 20+ years in the trades

Most software for trade businesses doesn't fail because it's badly built. It fails in the first month, quietly, because it asks you to rebuild your company inside it — new templates, new workflows, new habits for you and everyone who works with you. You're busy running jobs. The rebuild never finishes, the subscription keeps billing, and six months later you're back in Word and spreadsheets.

I've been on that ride. Before I built my own tool, I tried what was out there. These are the five questions I wish I'd asked every vendor before giving them a card number.

1. "Can I keep my own documents?"

Your proposal took years to get right. It sounds like you, your customers recognize it, and it wins work. Ask the vendor directly: does your tool use my document, or do I have to move my business into your templates? If the answer involves the word "rebuild," you now know what your first month looks like.

The test: hand them a real proposal you sent last month and ask, "What does this look like in your system?" The answer tells you everything.

2. "What does my crew have to learn?"

A tool the office loves and the field ignores is a tool half your company isn't using. If the guys in the truck need training sessions to clock time or open a work ticket, it won't survive a busy season. The field side should work like a phone app they already know — a few big buttons, no manual.

3. "What happens to what my customer sees?"

Some tools send your customers a branded portal link, a strange PDF layout, or an invoice that doesn't look like it came from you. Your paperwork is part of your reputation. Ask to see exactly what a customer receives — the document, the email, all of it — and ask whose logo and formatting is on it.

4. "How does it get along with my books?"

If you run QuickBooks, the tool should meet your books where they are — invoices and payments matching, not a second set of numbers you reconcile by hand every month. "We have an export" is not an integration. Ask what syncs, in which direction, and what you'll still have to type twice.

5. "What does it cost when I add a person?"

Per-seat pricing punishes you for growing — add a tech, add your spouse doing the books, and the bill climbs. Ask for the price with every person who'll ever touch it counted in. If the number moves every time your company does, you'll spend the next year rationing logins.

The pattern behind all five

Every one of these questions is really the same question: does the software bend to how my business already works, or do I bend to it? You've spent years figuring out how to run your company. The right tool respects that. The wrong one costs you a subscription and a rebuild you never had time for.
