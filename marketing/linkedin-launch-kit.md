# TradeGOAT — LinkedIn Launch Kit

Everything you need to stand up a LinkedIn presence for TradeGOAT and start
promoting it this week. Copy is written in TradeGOAT's own voice and pulled
straight from the site, so most of this is fill-in-the-blank, not
write-from-scratch.

**The one thing to understand first:** for an early-stage B2B tool, *your
personal profile out-earns the company page roughly 5-to-1.* LinkedIn's feed
favors people over brands, and "a 20-year contractor who got fed up and built
the tool himself" is the most persuasive thing TradeGOAT has going for it. So we
build **both** — the Company Page as the official home, your profile as the
engine. Post from *you*, and re-share to the page.

## Part 3 — Who to connect with (your first 100)

Reach on LinkedIn is downstream of your network. Spend week one building it:

- **Every contractor and trade owner you already know** — elevator, HVAC,
  electrical, plumbing, mechanical, GC. These are your buyers *and* your best
  early pilots.
- **General contractors and PMs** you've worked with (they feel the proposal /
  qualification-packet pain from the other side).
- **QuickBooks ProAdvisors and bookkeepers** who serve trades — natural
  referral partners.
- **Trade-association and supply-house people** in your region.

Rule of thumb: send 15–20 personalized connection requests a day (LinkedIn
throttles bulk requests). One line is enough: *"Fellow trades guy — building
something for contractors and would love to have you in my corner."*

## Part 5 — The ongoing rhythm

Keep it simple and sustainable. A working weekly cadence:

- **2–3 posts/week** from your profile. Rotate through four buckets:
  1. **Story** — something from 20 years in the trade (these travel furthest).
  2. **Problem** — a pain every owner feels; end with a question.
  3. **Product** — one feature, one screenshot, one outcome.
  4. **Proof** — a pilot signup, a milestone, a lesson from onboarding.
- **Comment more than you post.** 15 min/day replying on other contractors' and
  trades' posts does more for reach than a fourth post.
- **Reply to every comment** on your own posts within the first hour — early
  engagement is what the algorithm rewards.
- **Repost the winners** to the Company Page so it never looks empty.

**Do NOT** buy followers, use engagement pods, or auto-DM pitches on connect.
For a founder-led B2B launch it reads as spam and it's the fastest way to torch
the trust that "built by a contractor" earns you.

## Part 6 — 14-day launch checklist

**Day 1** — Optimize your personal profile (Part 2). Turn on Creator Mode.
**Day 2** — Create the Company Page (Part 1). Add logo, cover, button, tagline.
**Day 3** — Link your Founder role to the page. Add Featured links.
**Days 3–7** — Send 15–20 personalized connection requests/day (Part 3).
**Day 4** — Publish Post #1 (launch). Repost to the page. Reply to every comment.
**Day 6** — Post #2 (the problem).
**Day 8** — Post #3 (job scorecard + screenshot).
**Day 10** — Post #4 (build-in-public).
**Day 12** — Post #5 (early-access ask).
**Day 14** — Review: which post got the most reach? Do more of that. Repeat.
